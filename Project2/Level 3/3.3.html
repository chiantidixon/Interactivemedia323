It lies in our hands
in crystals too intricate to
decipher. It goes into the skillet without
being given a second thought. It spills on the floor
so fine we step all over it. We carry
a pinch behind each eyeball. It breaks out
out on our foreheads. We store it inside our
bodies in secret wineskins. At supper, we pass
pass it around the table talking of holidays and the sea.
