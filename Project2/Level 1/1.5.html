                                           It lies in
                                 our hands in crystals
                              too intricate to desipher.

















                                        It goes into
                                  the skillet without
                         being given a second thought.

















                                           It spills
                                     on the floor so
                              fine we step all over it.

















                                              We
                                      carry a pinch
                                behind each eyeball.

















                                               It
                                       breaks out
                                 on our foreheads.

















                                       We store it
                                   inside our bodies
                                in seceret wineskins.

















                                     At supper, we
                             pass it around the table,
                        talking of holidays and the sea.
